Her Side

 

We first met March 2003. He asked me if I wanted to see a movie with some free passes he acquired -- At first, I thought he might have messaged the wrong person(!) but I said yes and agreed to meet this "legend". At the time, it felt like so many of my friend circles/cousins/brother already knew Paul through different parts of University life/ski racing but it wasn't until our first date that we had formally met. We hit it off right away despite our quite different personalities and life experiences!!

 

In the early days, we spent a lot of time around the University of Calgary and exploring wing night specials in Calgary. LOL.

​

Since then we've both experienced a lot of life together. We've traveled to over 34 countries (depends on who's counting!!) and lived in 3. In the time we've been together, we've also managed to accumulate 6 University degrees between us!

​

Paul asked me to marry him on December 31, 2018. We were sitting out on a dock on the beach in Waikiki, Hawaii watching the NYE fireworks when he asked. I thought he was joking so in complete shock I responded - "what?!!!" Shortly after I realized he was serious and of course emphatically said "yes"!!

 

I'm excited to see what the next chapter in life brings and to have such an amazing partner beside me to experience it with!

His Side

of ours was over in my dorm room at the time and emphatically said "That's Rob's
sister! Talk to her!". Luckily I had won two tickets to a horrible movie and somehow
convinced her to join me. She picked me up, bought me dinner, and endured the
movie with me. From that day she was a keeper.

We spent the remainder of our undergrads together, and when I moved down to
California to go to Stanford, Michelle joined me a few months later. We grew up
together learning how to live in this new country and do all the adult things (I
still haven't figured out laundry, but that's besides the point). We got our
first cell phones together, did our first apartment hunting, made our first
lifelong friends, went on our first couples travels, and countless more exciting
parts of this lifelong journey.

I'm incredibly lucky that she's willing to marry me 5959 days after the first
day we met. I hope her endurance continues for many more oddly shaped multiples
of oddly chosen units.
